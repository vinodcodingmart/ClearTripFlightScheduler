<p>Jamnagar, also referred to as the ‘Jewel of Kathiawar’, is in the state of Gujarat. Once known as a small pearl-fishing village, it is now a beautiful and vibrant city that has managed to retain its distinct old-world charm.</p><p>Founded by Jam Raval in the year 1540, it was once the capital of the princely state of Nawanagar. The city draws a mix of religious tourists and nature enthusiasts, as it is known for its wildlife sanctuaries, mesmerizing beaches, untouched islands, temples, and palaces. Some of the must-visit places in Jamnagar include Bhujio Kotho, Kotha Bastion, Pratap Vilas Palace, Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary, Balachadi Beach, and Solarium.</p><p>The city is home to a number of local crafts, such as bandhani work, tie and die fabric dyeing, silver engraving, and jewelry making. This makes Jamnagar an important city for commerce as well.</p><p>The easiest and fastest way to reach Jamnagar is by air. The <a href="https://www.cleartrip.com/tourism/airports/jamnagar-airport.html" target="_blank">Jamnagar Airport</a> also referred as Govardhanpur Airport is located in the city itself. The airport is owned by the Indian Air Force, but it allows both the private and commercial flights. The Jamnagar Airport operates from a single terminal, which has a capacity of 75 each for departures and arrivals.</p><p>Travelling to and from the airport is easy, as the bus station is only 7 km from the airport. Cabs and auto-rickshaws are also easily available in the city, as well as outside the airport. The railway station is also conveniently located, at a distance of 9 km from the airport.</p><p>Travelers are advised to avoid traveling during summers as the heat gets stifling and you should wear light and airy clothes if you must visit the city at this time of the year.</p>
