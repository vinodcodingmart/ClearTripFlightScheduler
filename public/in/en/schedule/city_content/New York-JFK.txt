<p>To most of the world, New York is synonymous with modernity, city life, and progress. The city that never sleeps, the place where dreams can come true and a metropolis that is always buzzing with excitement, New York City, or the Big Apple, as it is fondly called, is the one place on earth that everyone wants to visit at least once in their lifetime. As a tourist, a visit to this historic city will certainly be life changing and memorable to say the least. It is also a leading business destination and is many ways the center of global commerce, as it is home to the world’s largest stock exchanges, the New York Stock Exchange and NASDAQ.</p><p>Art, culture, entertainment, architecture, fashion, food, beauty, and history are just some of the many attractions that New York has to offer. This concrete jungle opens up its doors to several opportunities all at once. At night, the city’s skyline twinkles brightly like a million stars in the night sky.</p><p>You can easily reach this hub of international trade and fashion as there are three international airports in New York City – John F Kennedy International Airport (JFK), Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR), and La Guardia International Airport (LGA).</p><p>The <a href="https://www.cleartrip.com/tourism/airports/new-york-airport.html" target="_blank">John F Kennedy International Airport</a> is the main international airport, located in the southeast part of the city. It is approximately 20 km from Lower Manhattan. It is one of the busiest airports in the world, catering to both domestic and international flights. There are 6 terminals at the airport - Terminals 1, 2, 4, 5, 7, and 8. To ensure easy travel between terminals you can make use of the AirTrain service. This service also links the airport to the New York City Subway, in Queens, and Long Island Rail Road.</p><p>The Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) is one of the oldest airports in the United States. The airport offers both domestic and international flight services. There are three terminals at the airport – A, B, and C. The third airport, the LaGuardia Airport (LGA) has four passenger terminals – Terminals A, B, C, and D.</p><p>It is easy to travel between the airports and to various locations within the city, as all three airports can be reached by rental cars, taxis, subway metro, and buses.</p><p>A helpful tip for tourists in New York would be to confirm your terminal with your airline, as there is a risk of missing your flight because of the confusion that can arise with 3 different airports and their numerous terminals.</p>
